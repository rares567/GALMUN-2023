TOPIC - Regulating the activity of pharmaceutical multinational corporations

In present days, the global pharmaceutical industry, or Big Pharma, still stands unchallenged in its ability to dictate government health policies, ability comparable to that of a state.

While hiking up the price of drugs is not as of now illegal, it surely points towards a question of morality. People all over the world suffer because of this unjust system in which, if they are not from a privileged class, they simply cannot treat themselves of otherwise treatable diseases. On the other hand, companies or people who hike up these prices do so because they are expected by their investors to maximize their profit. But is money really worth these peopleâ€™s lives?

Looking at other problems, like large laboratories concealing or playing down results of their clinical trials from the health authorities, it is clear as day that the system is in need of a change.

Are you ready to find solutions? If so, the WHO committee awaits you!

About the Committee

The World Health Organization is the directing and coordinating authority on international health within the United Nations system. The objective of WHO is the attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health. Health, as defined in the WHO Constitution, is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.
