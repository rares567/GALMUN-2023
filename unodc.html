TOPIC - Tackling the issue of the opioid crisis

For over a decade, the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) has made continuous efforts with the voice of science to help put a stop to addiction to medicine. The opioid crisis represents an increasing global problem, one linked to high mortality rates, the consequences of which affects millions. Nevertheless, many cannot afford proper care for illnesses, even though access to opioid analgesics has been provided by international drug conventions.

In more recent years, this problem has started spreading worldwide. Overdoses related to opiates have skyrocketed on the other side of the Atlantic. While opioid related deaths are currently at significantly lower levels than in the USA, they still represent an avertible health and financial burden across the EU countries. In Africa, an opiate that goes under the name of Tramadol is quickly getting people hooked, especially those who are part of the lower class in rural areas. Across the Pacific, Fentanyl produced in China is making its way to powerful drug cartels in Mexico and other South American countries, which lace their drugs with it, making them more addictive, only to then export them to the USA. In Oceania, Australia and New Zealand are fighting a gruesome battle against the illicit import of opiates from South East Asian countries like Myanmar, India, Laos, and even from Afghanistan, the world's largest opium production.

So, what are you waiting for? Join us in the UNODC committee to engage in fruitful debates on how to stop one of the 21st century's most dangerous crises and how to aid the victims of drug abuse.

About the Committee

The United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) has the mandate to make the world safer from drugs, organized crime, corruption and terrorism. The organization is committed to achieving health, security and justice for all by tackling these threats and promoting peace and sustainable well-being as deterrents to them.
